Broad Cast Method

This is the most common method of fertilizer application,
where the fertilizer is spread uniformly over the entire field surface. Broadcasting can be done by hand or with a mechanical spreader.

Learn more

Top Dressing and Side dressing

Spreading of fertilizer in standing crops without considering the crop rows is termed as a
top dressing. However, when the crop rows are taken into account, and the material is dropped on the ground surface near the crop rows, it is called
side dressing.

Learn more

Plough sole placement

When the fertilizers are applied in open furrows at plow sole level while plowing,
it is termed plow sole placement. Such furrows are covered immediately during the next run of the plow.

Irrigation and Water Conservation Techniques

Surface Irrigation

Surface irrigation is a type of irrigation method that involves
applying water to the soil surface and allowing it to infiltrate and move through the soil by gravity. Surface irrigation can be divided into several subtypes, including:

  • Flood Irrigation
  • Furrow Irrigation
  • Basin Irrigation
  • Border Irrigation

Learn more

Drip Irrigation

SDrip irrigation is a type of irrigation method that involves
applying water directly to the roots of plants through a network of small tubes or emitters.
Drip irrigation systems can be divided into several subtypes, including:

  • Point source drip
  • Subsurface drip
  • Micro-sprinkler

Learn more

Manual Irrigation

Manual irrigation refers to the application of water to crops by hand or with simple tools like
buckets, watering cans, or hoses. This type of irrigation is typically used for
small gardens, smallholder farms, or areas where water is scarce, and more advanced irrigation systems are not available.

Fertilizers

Organic Fertilizers

Organic fertilizers are derived from natural sources and contain nutrients that are released slowly and in a more natural form.
They are commonly used in organic agriculture to improve soil fertility and crop growth.
Here are some examples of organic fertilizers:

  • Compost
  • Manure
  • Fish Emulsion
  • Bone meal
  • Blood meal

Learn more

Inorganic Fertilizers

Inorganic fertilizers, also known as synthetic or chemical fertilizers,
are manufactured using chemical processes and contain nutrients in an easily available form for plants.
They are commonly used in modern agriculture to improve crop growth and increase yields.
Here are some examples of inorganic fertilizers:

  • Nitrogen Fertilizers
  • Phosphorus Fertilizers
  • Potassium Fertilizers
  • Calcium Fertilizers
